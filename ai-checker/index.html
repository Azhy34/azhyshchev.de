AI VISIBILITY GUIDE

What is AI Visibility and Why Does Your Website Need It?

AI visibility means whether your website can be found and read by AI-powered search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. These systems do not work like traditional search engines — they send a single HTTP request, read the raw HTML, and move on. If your content is rendered by JavaScript, they see an empty page.

Businesses with low AI visibility miss out on a fast-growing traffic channel. When someone asks ChatGPT "best AI automation consultant in Munich" or Perplexity "how to automate invoices in Germany", only websites that AI crawlers can actually read appear in the answers. This is where generative engine optimization (GEO) becomes critical.

How AI Search Engines Read Websites

AI crawlers — GPTBot (ChatGPT), ClaudeBot (Anthropic), PerplexityBot — work differently from Googlebot. They do not execute JavaScript. They fetch the raw HTML of a page in a single request. If your site uses React, Vue, or Angular without server-side rendering (SSR), the AI crawler sees a blank shell with no content.

Three technical signals determine whether an AI crawler can index your site: server-side rendering (content in the initial HTML), an open robots.txt that permits AI bots, and an llms.txt file that tells AI systems what your site is about. This free AI readiness checker audits all three in 10 seconds.

What is Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)?

Generative engine optimization (GEO) is the practice of making websites readable and citable by AI language models. Unlike classic SEO which optimizes for keyword ranking in blue links, GEO optimizes for citation in AI-generated answers. Answer engine optimization (AEO) is a related term used specifically for voice search and conversational AI platforms.

The core GEO checklist: server-side rendering so AI crawlers can read your content, structured data (Schema.org) so AI understands your entities, an llms.txt file as a machine-readable site summary, and a sitemap so crawlers can discover all your pages. This AI readiness checker scores your site across 8 GEO metrics instantly.

How do I make my website visible to ChatGPT and Perplexity?
To make your website visible to ChatGPT (GPTBot) and Perplexity (PerplexityBot), you need three things: (1) server-side rendering so AI crawlers can read your content without executing JavaScript, (2) an open robots.txt that explicitly allows GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot, and (3) an llms.txt file that gives AI systems a summary of your site. What is the difference between SEO and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?
Classic SEO optimizes for ranking in traditional search results — blue links ranked by keyword relevance and backlinks. Generative engine optimization (GEO) optimizes for citation in AI-generated answers from ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. GEO focuses on technical crawlability (SSR, llms.txt), structured data (Schema.org FAQPage, HowTo), and authoritative content that AI models cite as sources. A site can rank well on Google but be completely invisible to AI systems.

Um Ihre Website fur KI-Suche (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) zu optimieren, sind drei Schritte entscheidend: (1) Serverseitiges Rendering (SSR) statt reinem JavaScript-Rendering, damit KI-Crawler Ihre Inhalte lesen koennen.
